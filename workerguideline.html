Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Mandatory gear for all collection shifts

  • Gloves (Puncture resistant)
  • Reflective High-Vis Vest
  • Safety N95 Mask
  • Daily Inspection Required
  • Steel-toe Boots

All field workers are required to wear complete PPE before boarding the collection truck. Incomplete gear is grounds for temporary removal from duty until compliant.

1
Inspect all PPE items for damage or wear before each shift. Replace damaged items immediately through the LGU supply office.
2
Wear puncture-resistant gloves at all times when handling waste bags. Double-glove when handling medical or sharp waste.
3
Reflective vests must be worn during all roadside operations, especially during low-visibility or night conditions.
4
N95 masks are mandatory when handling organic, chemical, or unknown waste. Surgical masks do not meet this requirement.
5
Steel-toe boots protect against dropped containers and moving truck parts. Open-toed footwear is strictly prohibited on site.

Hazardous Waste Handling

Safe identification and disposal of chemicals/sharps

  • Segregate at source
  • Use sealed containers
  • Report spills immediately
  • Color-coded disposal

Hazardous waste includes medical sharps, chemical containers, batteries, and unknown liquids. Never mix hazardous materials with regular solid waste.

1
Identify hazardous waste by the orange hazard sticker or biohazard symbol. When in doubt, treat as hazardous.
2
Use only sealed, puncture-proof containers for sharps. Never recap needles by hand — use one-handed scoop technique.
3
Chemical spills: evacuate a 5-meter radius, alert your supervisor immediately, and do not attempt cleanup without proper training.
4
Color code: Red = infectious/medical, Yellow = chemical, Black = general hazardous, Green = biodegradable safe.
5
All hazardous waste must be logged in the collection manifest before transport. Unlogged hazmat is a compliance violation.

Emergency Response Procedures

What to do during spills, injuries, or accidents

  • Stop work immediately
  • Use onboard first-aid kit
  • Notify LGU Emergency Line
  • Document with photos

In any emergency — vehicle accident, worker injury, chemical spill, or fire — follow this response sequence without exception. Speed and sequence both matter.

1
Immediately stop all work and secure the area. Turn on hazard lights if near a roadway. Keep bystanders at a safe distance.
2
Assess injuries. Apply first aid from the onboard kit for minor wounds. Do not move injured persons with possible spinal injury.
3
Call the LGU Emergency Hotline: 0917-LGU-DAET. State your name, truck ID, exact location, and nature of emergency.
4
Document the scene with photos before any cleanup. Photos must be submitted within 2 hours to the LGU online portal.
5
Complete the Incident Report Form (IRF-01) available in the truck glovebox. Submit to the supervisor before end of shift.

Safe Collection Practices

Lifting techniques and route safety

  • Proper body mechanics
  • Avoid overloading trucks
  • Follow real-time schedule
  • 3-point contact on truck

Musculoskeletal injuries from improper lifting are the most common worker injuries. Following these techniques reduces risk by over 70%.

1
Always bend at the knees, not the waist. Keep the load close to your body and lift with your legs, not your back.
2
Never lift a waste bag over 25kg alone. Use a two-person lift for heavy containers. Use the wheeled cart for bins over 50kg.
3
Do not overload the truck beyond its rated capacity. Overloading causes brake failure, tire blowouts, and spill incidents.
4
Maintain 3-point contact (two hands, one foot or two feet, one hand) when mounting or dismounting the truck at all times.
5
Follow the assigned route in the EcoMate schedule. Route deviations require supervisor approval and must be logged in the app.
